Dinosaur Extinction Dinosaur Extinction

Dinosaurs went extinct about 66 million years ago.
The cause was a massive asteroid that hit somewhere around what is now Mexico.
All but the avian dinosaurs went extinct but prior to the extinction event, an estimated 600-1100 non avian dinosaur species existed.
No one knows if they were thriving or declining at the time.
A sudden increase in irradiated materials and an increase in shocked quartz (made once a large shockwave hits) supports the theory of the giant asteroid hitting.
